# Meaning of the Title

"Itadakimasu" (いただきます) is the phrase spoken before eating in Japanese. (ittadakimasu bismillah lol)

Normally it means:
"I humbly receive this meal."
The song intentionally twists this innocent phrase into something horrifying.
Instead of thanking someone for food...
...the protagonist appears to say it before consuming another person.
This immediately establishes food as both a literal and symbolic motif.

# Story Timeline

## Stage 1 - Isolation

The protagonist is introduced as someone who:
- cries in front of a mirror
- cannot finish meals
- feels disconnected from herself
- believes nobody notices her

Important lyrics:
"I left a little on the plate."
"You don't look like me."
These suggest severe loneliness and identity issues.

---

## Stage 2 - School

Lyrics describe:
- empty classroom
- missing school
- homework arriving by mail

Most interpretations conclude she is absent from school for an extended period.

Possible reasons include:
- depression
- illness
- social withdrawal

Nothing is officially confirmed.
The important point is that she believes the classroom continues normally without her.
She feels replaceable.

---

## Stage 3 - Meeting the Butterfly Girl

The music video introduces another girl wearing a butterfly hair clip.
Unlike everyone else, she spends time (for like, 100 frames in the 30fps mv) with the protagonist.

Scenes include:
- walking together hand-by-hand

This appears to be the protagonist's first meaningful friendship. (again, i guess)

---

## Stage 4 - Obsession

The protagonist begins viewing affection differently.
Love slowly becomes possession.
Food imagery becomes increasingly common.
Instead of expressing emotions normally, she describes them using cooking vocabulary.

Examples include:
- chewing
- swallowing
- recipes
- seasoning
- frying
Love is gradually transformed into food.

---

## Stage 5 - Violence

Near the climax, the lyrics become significantly darker.
Examples:

"You want to breathe, don't you?"
"Standing on tiptoe, you can only struggle."
These lyrics strongly imply suffocation.
Possible interpretations include:

### Hanging

Evidence:

- "want to breathe"
- "standing on tiptoe"
- struggling

Someone suspended by the neck instinctively stands on tiptoe while attempting to breathe.
This interpretation matches the physical wording remarkably well.
However, the MV never explicitly confirms hanging.

---

### Strangulation

The same lyrics also fit manual strangulation.
A victim being choked may:
- stand on tiptoe
- claw for air
- struggle to inhale
The lyrics intentionally avoid specifying the exact method.

---

### General Suffocation

The safest conclusion is simply:
The victim is suffocating.
Whether caused by hanging or strangulation is left ambiguous.

---

## Stage 6 - "Itadakimasu"

The title reaches its true meaning.
The protagonist appears to consume the butterfly girl.

Whether this is:
- literal cannibalism
- symbolic possession
is intentionally unclear.

The music video strongly implies literal murder.
The lyrics emphasize emotional possession.
Both readings coexist.

---

## Stage 7 - Aftermath

The butterfly girl disappears.
Television imagery appears.
Later lyrics reference TV reporting.

Possible interpretation:
The television reports the disappearance or murder. <- im unsure
The world continues normally.
Nobody notices until after everything has happened.

# Mirror Symbolism

Mirrors appear repeatedly.

Beginning:
"You don't look like me."

Ending:
"It seems like you were me all along."

This progression suggests identity collapse.
Instead of accepting herself...
...the protagonist erases the boundary between herself and another person.

---

# Butterfly Symbolism

Butterflies commonly represent:
- transformation
- fragile happiness
- life
- death
- the soul

The butterfly girl symbolizes something beautiful but temporary.
The protagonist cannot accept losing something beautiful.
Therefore she attempts to possess it forever.

---

# Food Symbolism

Food represents emotional attachment. (i guess)
Examples:

Eating
- accepting another person

Chewing
- repeatedly dwelling on memories

Cooking
- preparing for violence

Recipe
- the method of permanently keeping someone

Seasoning
- rationalizing obsession

Finishing a meal
- completing possession

---

# Analysis of "You want to breathe, don't you?"

This lyric is one of the darkest in the song.
Interpretation:

The speaker acknowledges the victim's instinct to survive.
Instead of helping...
she simply observes.
The line demonstrates complete emotional detachment.
It is no longer love.
It has become control.

---

# Analysis of "Standing on tiptoe, you can only struggle."

This lyric likely depicts the victim's final resistance.
Possible meanings:

Physical:

- hanging
- strangulation
- suffocation

Psychological:
The victim desperately tries to preserve her identity against the protagonist's obsession.
Both readings fit the themes of the song.

---

# Identity Collapse

The protagonist's progression:
"I am alone." -> "I need someone." -> "I don't want to lose you." -> "You can't leave." -> "You are mine." -> "You were me all along."
This final stage represents the disappearance of the boundary between self and other.

---

# Central Theme

The song is not primarily about cannibalism.
Cannibalism functions as the language used to describe obsessive attachment.
The real horror comes from:

- emotional dependency
- fear of abandonment
- distorted love
- loss of identity
- possession replacing affection

The protagonist mistakes ownership for intimacy.
Instead of wanting to be loved...
...she wants another person to become part of herself forever.

# Conclusion

"Itadakimasu" is a psychological horror work disguised as a song about food.

Every major motif:

- eating
- recipes
- cooking
- chewing
- swallowing

is ultimately about attachment.
The protagonist begins as someone desperate to be noticed.
Unable to understand healthy love, she concludes that the only way to ensure someone never leaves is to erase the boundary between "you" and "me."
The title, "Itadakimasu," transforms from a polite expression of gratitude into the final declaration of obsessive possession.
Rather than being a story solely about murder or cannibalism, the song explores how loneliness and fear of abandonment can distort love into something destructive.
